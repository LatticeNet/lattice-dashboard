## Design Context

### Users
Lattice is used by self-hosting operators, small infrastructure teams, and
technical founders running their own fleet control plane. They open the console
to understand node health, geographic placement, routing readiness, DNS/proxy
state, approvals, and operational risk without wading through raw logs.

### Brand Personality
Calm, precise, infrastructure-grade. Lattice should feel trustworthy and
purpose-built for repeated operational use, not like a marketing dashboard.
Every screen should help an operator move from signal to action quickly.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dense, refined operator console with deep slate-indigo surfaces, restrained
brand accents, crisp tabular data, and deliberate visual moments only when they
carry operational meaning. Map and topology views may be more visual, but must
stay inspectable, CSP-safe, and free of external tile dependencies.

### Design Principles
- Prefer real operational signal over decoration.
- Keep primary workflows visible, with advanced edits behind clear controls.
- Use compact, stable layouts that scan well on desktop and adapt without
  dropping critical controls on mobile.
- Treat empty, partial, and degraded data states as first-class UX.
- Never imply support for a server or agent capability that does not exist.
